During the second half of the nineteenth century, the field of polar science was in full swing.

In 1878, the Viennese polar scientist Josef Chavanne published a collection of monographs, articles and notes on the state of science in the polar regions. Supported by the Royal Geographical Society of Vienna, Chavanne had consulted with European societies and geographers to compile an extensive collection that spanned biography, history, ethnology, botany, geology or glaciology.
